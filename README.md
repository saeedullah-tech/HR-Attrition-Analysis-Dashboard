HR Attrition Analysis Dashboard | Power BI

An interactive HR Analytics Dashboard built in Power BI to uncover employee attrition patterns, identify high-risk employee segments, and provide data-driven insights that support workforce retention strategies.

📌 Project Overview

Employee attrition is one of the biggest challenges faced by HR departments. High turnover increases recruitment costs, reduces productivity, and impacts organizational performance.

This dashboard analyzes HR employee data to identify the major factors influencing attrition, including:

Employee demographics
Job roles
Salary bands
Job levels
Education fields
Work-life balance
Environment satisfaction
Years at the company

The dashboard enables HR professionals and business leaders to quickly identify trends and make informed retention decisions.

Key Business Insights
1. Salary is the strongest attrition driver
Employees earning up to 5K account for approximately 69% of total attrition, indicating compensation is a significant retention factor.
2. Entry-level employees leave the most
60.34% of attrition comes from Entry-Level employees.
Early-career employees represent the highest turnover segment.
3. Critical retention window
Most employees leave during their first two years with the company.
The attrition curve declines significantly after this period.
4. Highest-risk job roles

The highest employee turnover occurs among:

Laboratory Technician
Sales Executive
Research Scientist
Sales Representative
5. Age group with highest attrition

Employees aged 26–35 years represent the largest proportion of attrition (116 employees), making this the primary retention target.

6. Gender analysis
Male employees account for 64.13% of attrition.
Female employees account for 35.87%.
7. Work-Life Balance

Employees reporting Good work-life balance still account for the highest attrition volume, suggesting additional factors such as salary, career progression, or workload may influence employee turnover.

8. Environment Satisfaction

Employees with Low Environment Satisfaction experience the highest attrition, highlighting workplace environment as a key retention factor.
